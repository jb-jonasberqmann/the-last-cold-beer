### What the game is

A real-time mobile party game played at a real Danish summerhouse by a group of friends split into two teams. Players physically walk around the actual summerhouse property solving puzzles on their phones — the game and reality are intertwined. Think escape room meets drinking game, played in a real location.

The game takes place over one real evening and unfolds in three acts:
- **Act 1 — The Arrival:** Daytime. Everyone arrives outside. A friend called Mads is late and has the key. The group is stuck outside exploring the garden, terrace, and shed while they wait.
- **Act 2 — Settling In:** Evening. Inside the house. Cozy but something feels off. An old radio in the dining room has been crackling all night. The group explores rooms and tries to fix the radio. When they finally do, it broadcasts one unsettling line before overloading and cutting all power.
- **Act 3 — The Late Night:** Deep night. The house is completely dark. The group works through the property — inside and outside — with flashlights and candles, repairing broken things, discovering rooms they never explored, and eventually returning to the living room for the final reveal.

**The reveal:** One player was randomly chosen at the start as "the culprit" — the person who drank the last cold beer without replacing it. They don't know. Nobody knows. At the end of Act 3, the game reveals who it was. That person has to drink the last cold beer.

### The real location

**Nørrestrand 48A, Hou, Hals, North Jutland, Denmark**

A classic Danish summerhouse built in 1960, renovated in 2021. Red-brown horizontal timber cladding, low-pitched roof, white window frames. Sits on a large fenced natural plot — coastal Danish grass, low shrubs, open sky. The sea is 450 metres away. Flat landscape, light wind always present.

**Outside the house:** Gravel driveway, carport, large garden, petanque court, tool shed, new wooden terrace with sun loungers, glass conservatory/sunroom attached to the house, key box mounted beside the front door.

**Inside the house:** Living room with wood-burning stove, dining room with an old analog radio on a sideboard, kitchen, activity room (table tennis, darts, foosball, mini billiards), 3 bedrooms (a double room, a single room, a bunk room), 2 bathrooms.

### Visual style

**The single most important reference:** A Nordic travel poster crossed with a Scandinavian crime novel cover. Hand-illustrated feel, atmospheric, slightly worn. Not cartoon. Not photorealistic. Somewhere between painterly and illustrated — like a very good book cover.

**Mood references:**
- Stranger Things (seasons 1–2) — the warmth, the darkness, the creeping wrongness
- Midsommar — the lighting only, not the horror
- Nordic Noir book covers
- Old Danish tourism posters — the colour palette and slightly romantic illustration style

**Colour by act:**
- Act 1: Warm amber, golden hour, deep greens, gravel grey, wood tones. Cheerful but with one note that's slightly wrong.
- Act 2: Evening interior warmth — lamp yellows, wood paneling, 1960s furniture tones. Gradually cooler as the radio malfunctions.
- Act 3: Near-total darkness. Only pools of light — flashlight beam (warm amber), candle (orange flicker), phone screen (cold blue-white). High contrast. Everything familiar, made unfamiliar.

---

### Composition rule for all room backgrounds

Every room background must follow this rule — no exceptions:

- **Top third of the frame:** The main subject. The most important visual element goes here. This is what the player looks at.
- **Middle third:** Detail fades, atmosphere takes over. Soft transition.
- **Bottom third:** Fades completely to near-black. Nothing important here. A semi-transparent quest panel overlays this zone in the app — it must be dark enough to read text over.

Think of it like a film still where the subject's face is always in the top third and the bottom of the frame is shadow.

---

### Technical requirements for all assets

- **Room backgrounds:** 1080 × 1920px, vertical (portrait), opaque. Composition rule above applies to all.
- **Boss portraits:** 600 × 600px, square. White or transparent background — the subject only, no background scene.
- **Artifact illustrations:** 400 × 400px, square. Subject on white or transparent background — clean object illustration, no background scene.
- **Map illustrations:** Vertical, hand-drawn top-down style showing the property layout for each act. Nodes (rooms) shown as organic hand-drawn blobs connected by illustrated paths — like a treasure map or hand-sketched site plan.
- **UI elements:** Always on transparent/white background. Object or frame only.

---

### Style consistency rule

Once we establish the style with the first image, every subsequent image must match it exactly. If the first image uses a specific illustration technique, colour treatment, or level of detail — all following images must be consistent. We are building one cohesive visual world, not a collection of individual illustrations.

I will tell you when I'm happy with the style. After that, do not deviate from it.
